About

Min Sitt Thu Aung

Min Sitt started his design journey in his junior year of college by picking an app he loved — Snackpass — and creating a case study to improve their rewards program. He posted it across forums to gather user feedback, but to his surprise, it caught the attention of the CEO, who responded with positive feedback on his framework. Inspired and motivated, Min Sitt committed fully to the product and design world.

Since then, he has worked as a contract designer primarily at early-stage startups, completing 15 projects across a variety of industries — healthcare, fintech, SaaS, and more. Growing up in Myanmar and navigating an entirely new culture, environment, and set of fields he had never encountered before gave him something most designers don't have: the instinct to adapt. He explored data science, consulting, and accounting not out of indecision, but out of genuine curiosity. That breadth now makes him a designer with sharp product sense and analytical thinking. He can step into user research, product management, or design depending on what the team needs.

Now based in Chicago, Min Sitt is a product analyst in a rotational program at JP Morgan — actively leveraging those cross-functional skillsets and sharpening his design perspective through the lens of a product manager. He doesn't cling to ideas or get precious about solutions. He moves where the problem leads.
