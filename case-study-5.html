Case Study — 04

Shifting client service reps from reactive to proactive

The platform's transaction feature was almost entirely unused — despite transaction issues driving a significant share of all incoming cases. We set out to find out why, and redesign the experience from the ground up.

Role Product Designer
Type Internal Product
Company JP Morgan

Project Overview

My Role

Product Designer embedded in a cross-functional team of researchers, engineers, and product managers. I led design from discovery through handoff, facilitating user interviews, running usability testing sessions, and aligning with engineering on technical feasibility throughout the process.

Context

JP Morgan has an internal platform that is designed to be the one-stop shop for client service representatives — the people fielding calls, resolving payment issues, and maintaining relationships with the firm's most important clients. One of its features gives reps a view into their clients' transaction activity. Despite being built for exactly the kind of work reps do every day, it was almost entirely unused.

The Problem

Despite being available, the transaction feature had almost no adoption. Transaction-related issues account for a significant portion of all incoming case volume. That gap was costing reps time, clients trust, and the firm revenue.

The Outcome

We redesigned the experience around a time-based filtering model — surfacing only the transactions stuck in processing longer than expected. The solution is projected to save reps significant time per week, reclaim hundreds of thousands of hours firm-wide, and generate substantial multi-year value.
